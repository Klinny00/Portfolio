This is an app concept created to help pet owners keep track of their pets schedules and book appointments. Booking appointments for pets in new places may make owners feel hesitant. To mitigate this issue, this application provides a user-friendly and trustworthy environment for pet owners.

User Research

Method

Target User: Pet owners with experience in scheduling appointments for their pets. Owners that need of a way to track their pets schedules and want to provide their pet with the best care.


Method (Observation): I individually interviewed multiple pet owners in order to gain knowledge of their experience and hesitations with booking and keeping track of appointments. I made sure to interview a variety of pet owners such as people that own different species or have multiple pets.

Affinity Mapping

Efficient, user-friendly, and easy

An affinity cluster was formed to understand usual issues users could encounter, arising from the challenge in the problem statement. A total of 10 categories were established. These groups included: time, cost, informational, experts, scheduling, reviews, interface, personalization, reminders, and mood. The mood cluster signifies the user’s emotion while interacting with the app. The experts cluster points to the user’s confidence in their current vet or groomer avoiding misunderstandings, while the other group titles communicate their own meaning.

User Testings

User Testings

Afterwards, I created a paper prototype based on my ideations. Based off of user responses, I was able to narrow things down to a couple of important pages they need in order to reach their goal. After the creation of this low-fidelity, I conducted a user testing in which I had a few target users test out the prototype.

Testing Summary

The users liked the navigation and wire flow. However, there were a few issues in design cohesion, coherency, and spacing.
